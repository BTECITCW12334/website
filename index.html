Mortgage Advice

Buying a home in County Down involves several financial steps. Here is a simple guide to the process.

1. Deposits

Most lenders require a deposit of at least 5% to 10% of the property's value. Having a larger deposit often results in lower interest rates.

2. Agreement in Principle

Before viewing houses, it is helpful to get an Agreement in Principle from a lender. This shows how much you can afford to borrow.

3. Extra Costs

Remember to save for costs outside of your deposit, such as solicitor fees, survey fees, and stamp duty taxes.

4. Local Support

In Northern Ireland, options like Co-Ownership are available for those who cannot afford a full mortgage upfront.

Buying Guide

A simple 4-step guide to purchasing your home with True North Homes.

Step 1: Financial Preparation

Before viewing properties, ensure you have your deposit ready and a mortgage agreement in principle. This makes the buying process much faster.

Step 2: Property Viewings

Once you find a house you like, we will arrange a viewing. We recommend viewing a property at least twice—once during the day and once in the evening.

Step 3: Making an Offer

When you are ready, you can place an offer through us. We will communicate this to the seller and handle the negotiations for you.

Step 4: Legal & Completion

If your offer is accepted, you will need a solicitor to handle the legal paperwork (conveyancing). Once the contracts are signed and funds are transferred, you get your keys!
